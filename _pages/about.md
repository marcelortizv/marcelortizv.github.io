I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at [Emory University](https://economics.emory.edu/index.html){:target="_blank"}, where I am fortunate to be advised by [Pedro Sant’Anna](https://psantanna.com/){:target="_blank"}. I hold a B.A. and M.Sc. in Economics from [ESPOL](https://www.espol.edu.ec/){:target="_blank"}.

Before beginning my doctoral studies, I worked in the tech sector as Data Scientist and Machine Learning Engineer, and also served as a Research Consultant for the [Inter-American Development Bank](https://www.iadb.org/en){:target="_blank"}.

My research interests lie in applied and theoretical econometrics, particularly causal inference, statistical learning, and semiparametric efficiency theory. I aim to develop rigorous methods for addressing policy-relevant questions in applied microeconomics.
